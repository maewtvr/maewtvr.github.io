TYRANNOSAURUS REX

The Tyrannosaurus rex , often called "T. rex," was one of the largest terrestrial predators in history, reaching up to 40 feet long and 12 feet tall at the hips, with a strong tail for balance and muscular legs enabling speeds up to 20 mph. It lived during the late Cretaceous period (68–66 million years ago) in what is now North America, preying on large herbivores like Triceratops and scavenging when needed. Its skull, over 4 feet long, housed 60 serrated teeth that could grow up to 12 inches, allowing it to crush bones with a bite force of 12,800 pounds. Despite its small arms, T. rex was an apex predator with exceptional vision, a keen sense of smell, and likely some feathers during its juvenile stages. It went extinct with the other non-avian dinosaurs during the mass extinction event 66 million years ago.

Characteristics

Size: T. rex could grow up to 40 feet long, about 12–13 feet tall at the hips, and weigh around 7–9 tons. Skull: Its skull was over 4 feet long, equipped with 60 serrated teeth, some as large as 12 inches, ideal for crushing bones and tearing flesh. Arms: Despite its large size, T. rex had short but strong two-fingered arms, which were about 3 feet long and possibly used for gripping prey. Legs: Its muscular legs allowed for bursts of speed, estimated at 12–20 mph. Tail: The long, heavy tail balanced its massive body and helped it maneuver.

Behavior and Diet:

Carnivore: T. rex was an apex predator, preying on herbivores like Triceratops and Edmontosaurus, but it might also have scavenged when needed. Bite Force: It had one of the most powerful bites of any land animal, with a bite force of about 12,800 pounds. Hunting: It may have been a solitary hunter or hunted in pairs, although this is debated.

Fossil Discoveries

Fossils have been found in North America, particularly in the United States and Canada. The most complete T. rex skeleton, named "Sue", is housed at the Field Museum in Chicago.

Extinction:

T. rex, like all non-avian dinosaurs, went extinct about 66 million years ago, likely due to a massive asteroid impact and subsequent environmental changes.
